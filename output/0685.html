************* CALL FOR PAPERS - Elsevier Computer Networks - DEADLINE EXTENSION *************


                                 Special Issue on Information-Centric Networking (ICN)

www.journals.elsevier.com/computer-networks/call-for-papers/information-centric-networking/

*********************************************************************************************************************


Information Centric Networking (ICN, also referred to as Content-Centric Networking, or Name-Oriented Networking) is a new communication paradigm centered around named-data. Accordingly, network primitives (e.g., routing, forwarding, data transfer) are based on content names rather than host identifiers, and every node can potentially store content to serve user requests. ICN has received increasing attention in the last few years as it is considered a promising approach to overcome the emerging limitations of overlay infrastructures to deal with today's traffic trends. Indeed, ICN has the potential to natively address the challenge of distributing the large amount of content associated with different Internet services in a scalable, flexible, and efficient way.

This special issue invites novel contributions on ICN architecture and mechanism designs for different environments (e.g., Public Internet, MANET, virtual private network, cellular networks), performance evaluation and analysis, implementation and experimental evaluation. Submissions related to ICN deployment scenarios and business models are also welcome. Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:
-Naming and addressing
-Inter/intra domain name-based routing protocols
-High-speed name-based forwarding mechanisms
-Transport level algorithms and protocols
-Resource management, traffic control and traffic engineering
-Storage management and cache coordination strategies
-Content router design
-Security architecture and technologies
-Privacy and trust preserving mechanisms
-ICN legacy and new applications for multimedia, web services, mobile, cloud computing
-Services over ICN
-Mobility management
-Quality of Service
-Performance evaluation and analysis
-Implementation and practical experiences
-ICN testbeds and simulation frameworks
-ICN business models, deployment scenarios/use-cases and economic issues
-ICN regulatory policies and Intellectual Property Right issues

Submission format
The submitted papers must be clearly written in excellent English and describe original research which is not published nor currently under review by other journals or conferences. Author guidelines for preparation of manuscript can be found atwww.elsevier.com/locate/comnet/

Submission Guideline
All manuscripts and any supplementary material should be submitted through the Elsevier Editorial System (EES). The authors must select as "Information Centric Networking" when they reach the "Article Type" step in the submission process. The EES website is located at: http://ees.elsevier.com/comnet/

Guide for Authors
This site will guide you stepwise through the creation and uploading of you article. The guide for Authors can be found on the journal homepage (www.elsevier.com/comnet/). Papers are limited to twenty pages. The formatting of the paper for submission is single column, one and one-half or double spacing and 10 pt font size.
