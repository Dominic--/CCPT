Title: The Past, Present, and Future of Malware Defense
Time: 14:00-15:30, Dec. 29, 2011
Meeting Room: FIT 1-515
 
Abstract:
=======
Malware was, is and will continue to be a vexing problem for all of us.
In this talk, I will present a series of research activities that have
been conducted in my group to mitigate the malware threats, ranging from
defending against traditional kernel rootkits to uncovering zero-day mobile
malware.
 
Biography:
===========
Dr. Xuxian Jiang is an Assistant Professor in the Computer Science Department
and a core member of the Cyber Defense Lab at North Carolina State University,
Raleigh, NC, USA. He received his PhD degree in Computer Science from Purdue
University in 2006. His research interests are mainly in systems security,
including current focus in improving the privacy and security for mobile platforms
as well as the assurance for virtualization-based cloud computing infrastructures.
He is a recipient of a CAREER Award from the National Science Foundation (2010)
and the Best Paper Award from the Symposium on Recent Advances in Intrusion
Detection (2008). Jiang's research has been mainly supported by the NSF.
 
http://www.cs.ncsu.edu/faculty/jiang
